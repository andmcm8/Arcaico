Dignified Social Housing:
A New Standard

Redefining affordable living through community-centric design, abundant natural light, and high-quality materials.

The perception of social housing has long been marred by the brutalist giants of the mid-20th century—monolithic structures that prioritized density over dignity. Our latest project challenges this narrative, proving that affordability does not require a compromise on aesthetic or spatial quality.

Located on the outskirts of Lyon, the "Solaris" complex reimagines the communal living experience. By breaking down the scale of the development into interconnected, mid-rise volumes, we have created a village-like atmosphere that encourages interaction while preserving individual privacy. The rigorous geometric facade is softened by the use of warm timber cladding and extensive vertical greenery.

Central to the design philosophy was the concept of the "active threshold." Rather than dark, narrow corridors, circulation spaces are expanded into generous, light-filled galleries that serve as extensions of the living units—spaces for children to play and neighbors to converse.

format_quote

"True sustainability in architecture is not just about energy efficiency; it is about creating spaces that people are proud to call home, ensuring the longevity of the community itself."

— Marcus Thorne, Lead Architect

Materiality & Light

Cost constraints in social housing often lead to the specification of synthetic, short-lifespan materials. Conversely, we invested heavily in the building envelope, utilizing prefabricated cross-laminated timber (CLT) panels. This not only reduced construction time by 30% but also imbued the interiors with a natural warmth and superior acoustic performance rarely seen in this sector.

Floor-to-ceiling windows are standard in every unit, a design choice aimed at democratizing access to natural light. Deep balconies provide passive solar shading in the summer while extending the usable living space, blurring the line between indoor and outdoor environments.
