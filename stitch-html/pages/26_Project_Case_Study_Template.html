Project Narrative

A seaside sanctuary carved into the cliffside, where white concrete meets the raw power of the Pacific.

Ossis Villa redefines coastal living by embedding itself into the landscape rather than imposing upon it. The stark white concrete forms act as a canvas for the changing light of the ocean sky, while deep overhangs provide respite from the intense Malibu sun. The design prioritizes solitude and connection to the horizon.

Internally, the villa is a study in texture and restraint. Limestone floors flow seamlessly onto cantilevered terraces, blurring the line between the protected interior and the wild exterior. The architecture serves as a silent frame for the rhythmic motion of the waves below.

Design Philosophy
"We sought to create a structure that feels as though it was eroded by the wind and sea, yet stands firm against them—a permanent fossil in a shifting landscape."
